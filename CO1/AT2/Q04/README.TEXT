1. The upper layer establishes Image Acquisition Conditions, which are controlled by three main drivers: Environment & Scene, Imaging Hardware, and Operational Parameters.
2. The Environment & Scene node dictates external factors including illumination types, light spectrums (visible, IR, multispectral), scene complexity, dynamic ranges, weather variations, and shadows/specularities.
3. Concurrently, Imaging Hardware configures physical constraints through Sensor Type (CMOS, CCD, LiDAR), Pixel Size & Dynamic Range, Filter Arrays (Bayer, Polarizing), and Lens Properties (focal length, aperture, distortion).
4. These hardware pieces interact directly with Operational Parameters, where camera-side settings like Gain (ISO), Exposure Time (shutter speed), Aperture, Depth of Field, Light Intake, and Frame Rate are dynamically calibrated.
5. These three acquisition pillars directly determine Image Characteristics & Quality Metrics, splitting incoming data into positive qualities (Contrast, Spatial Resolution, Color Fidelity) and destructive anomalies (Color Artifacts, Motion Blur, Lens Artifacts).
6. These quality metrics filter down into Downstream Image Processing Tasks, where initial restoration algorithms execute Image Enhancement & Restoration (denoising, dehazing), Color Correction, Feature Extraction, Geometric Correction, and Calibration.7. Cleaned data arrays feed into the core layer of Computer Vision Algorithms & Models, powering cascading analytical stages: Object Detection (e.g., CNNs, YOLO, Faster R-CNN) $\rightarrow$ Segmentation (Semantic, Instance) $\rightarrow$ Tracking (temporal consistency) $\rightarrow$ 3D Reconstruction (SfM, SLAM).
8. The output efficiency from these computer vision models determines the final metrics of Application Performance, which are explicitly benchmarked across distinct operational axes: Accuracy, Speed, Precision, and F1-Score.
9. On the left side of the performance tier, these metrics establish baseline reliability scores for safety-critical civilian systems, specifically Autonomous Driving and Medical Diagnostics.
10. On the right side of the performance tier, these exact same metrics evaluate real-world production viability for specialized commercial tasks, including Face Recognition Systems, Surveillance & Security, and Robotic Grasping.
