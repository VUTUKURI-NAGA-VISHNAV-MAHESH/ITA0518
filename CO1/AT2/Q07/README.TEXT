1. The framework organizes Computer Vision into a clear hierarchy with three distinct tiers: Tier 1 (Low-Level), Tier 2 (Mid-Level), and Tier 3 (High-Level).

2. At the foundational layer, Tier 1: Low-Level Visual Processing operates purely on pixels and matrices, covering essential foundational tasks starting with Image Acquisition and Pixel Manipulations.

3. Tier 1 continues with Sensing & Denoising and Filtering & Enhancement, as well as Geometric Transformations which enable final spatial alignment via Homography & Warp.

4. Tier 1 explicitly cites foundational literature for edge detection, referencing Gonzalez & Woods for Sobel and Canny operators.

5. This low-level processing provides the input for Tier 2: Mid-Level Visual Analysis, which is defined by structural aggregation and creating representations from Tier 1 output.

6. Key processes in Tier 2 include Segmentation (Semantic, Instance) and Feature Extraction, and this layer cites iconic mathematical descriptors like SIFT and ORB by Lowe.

7. Tier 2 also analyzes motion and spatial structure through Optical Flow (citing Lucas-Kanade) and 3D Scene Reconstruction (SfM).

8. Mid-level analysis then abstracts these features, which Tier 3: High-Level Visual Interpretation uses to construct semantic understanding and reasoning.

9. Tier 3 utilizes the revolutionary performance of Deep Learning Backbones (CNNs, Transformers) and cites seminal breakthroughs such as AlexNet and ResNet by Krizhevsky et al. and He et al.

10. Finally, these high-level interpretations drive concrete Downstream Vision Applications, including Autonomous Driving, Medical Imaging, and Surveillance.
